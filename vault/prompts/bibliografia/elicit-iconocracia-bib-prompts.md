# Elicit iconocracia — bib prompts

## Prompt

```
# Prompts para Elicit — Mapeamento de Corpus
## Iconocracia: Alegoria Feminina na História da Cultura Jurídica (Séculos XIX–XX)

---

## 1. Rechtsikonographie / Iconografia Jurídica

- What role do visual representations of justice play in European legal culture?
- How has legal iconography (Rechtsikonographie) developed as a field of study?
- How are courthouses decorated with allegorical imagery and what does it signify?
- What is the relationship between visual symbols and legitimacy in legal institutions?
- How do images function as legal sources in the history of law?

---

## 2. Alegorias Femininas Nacionais

- How do female allegories like Marianne, Britannia, Germania, and Columbia function as national symbols?
- What political functions do female personifications of the nation serve in 19th and 20th century visual culture?
- How was the French Republic personified as a woman and what does Marianne represent?
- How did European allegorical models of nationhood transfer to postcolonial contexts like Brazil?
- What is the iconographic history of the Brazilian Republic's female effigy?
- How does Belgium use feminine allegory in state and legal imagery?
- What are the origins and political uses of Britannia as a national personification?
- How did Germania function as a symbol of German nationhood in the 19th century?
- How was Columbia used as a female allegory of the United States?

---

## 3. Iustitia / Lady Justice

- How has the iconography of Lady Justice (blindfold, sword, scales) evolved from antiquity to modernity?
- Why is justice personified as a female figure and what are the gendered implications?
- How do representations of Iustitia differ across courthouses in different national contexts?
- What is the history of the blindfold in depictions of Justice?
- How do statues of Justice in public spaces reflect legal and political culture?

---

## 4. Teoria Feminista e Alegoria

- How does feminist theory explain the use of female bodies to represent abstract political concepts like justice and liberty?
- What is the relationship between allegorical femininity and women's exclusion from political and legal rights?
- How does Carole Pateman's sexual contract theory apply to constitutional and visual analysis?
- How does Marina Warner's analysis of female allegory relate to political iconography?
- What does it mean that female allegories represent the state while real women are excluded as legal subjects?
- How does gender theory intersect with the study of national monuments and public art?

---

## 5. Iconologia / Panofsky / Método

- How has Panofsky's iconological method been applied outside art history, particularly in legal or political studies?
- What methods exist for visual analysis in legal history and how replicable are they?
- How can iconographic and iconological analysis be adapted for comparative legal studies?
- What is the relationship between iconography, iconology, and semiotics in visual culture studies?

---

## 6. Contestação e Reapropriação

- How have feminist movements contested or reappropriated national symbols and state monuments?
- What is the relationship between iconoclasm, monument destruction, and political resistance?
- How do protests use visual symbols to challenge state authority and patriarchal structures?
- How were state symbols contested during political crises like January 8 in Brazil?
- How do postcolonial societies deal with inherited colonial monuments and statues?

---

## 7. Pós-colonial e Circulação

- How do European legal and political symbols circulate and transform in postcolonial contexts?
- What role does visual culture play in postcolonial nation-building and gender hierarchies?
- How does colonial legal iconography differ from metropolitan models in Latin America?
- How were French republican symbols adapted in former colonies?

---

## 8. Global Legal History / Regimes de Normatividade

- What is global legal history and how does it approach normative knowledge production across jurisdictions?
- How do legal ideas and symbols circulate between European and Latin American legal cultures?
- How does Thomas Duve's framework of normative regimes apply to comparative legal history?

---

2. **Prioridade média** — Eixos 3 (Iustitia), 5 (Método), 6 (Contestação)
```
