# The Haptic Dimension — Touched Allegories

## Core idea

Coins are TOUCHED. The female allegorical body on currency is not just seen but HANDLED — held, folded, pocketed, accumulated. This is the most intimate state-body contact, and purely visual analysis misses it.

## Cases

- **UK-PENNY-1860**: hundreds of millions in daily circulation. Britannia touched more than any painting in history.
- **BR-2000R-1907**: white European face passing between Afro-Brazilian hands
- **US-EDUC-1896-05**: sensuous semi-nude Electricity that people HELD and FOLDED
- **BE-IND-1880**: La Belgique with Constitution — the social contract literally in your pocket

## Theoretical potential

- Haptic vs. optic: Riegl's distinction applied to state iconography
- The coin as the most democratic and most intimate medium of Feminilidade de Estado
- Touch as a form of the Contrato Sexual Visual that bypasses the gaze
