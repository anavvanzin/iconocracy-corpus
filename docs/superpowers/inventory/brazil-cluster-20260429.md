## 2. Individual File Assessments

### 2.1 Numismatic Core (Coins)

**SCOUT-316 — 500 Réis República, 1889**
Date: 1889 | Medium: Moeda (prata .917) | Regime: FUNDACIONAL
Description: First republican coinage. Busto feminino with barrete frígio and laurel wreath, in strict profile. Inscription: "REPUBLICA DOS ESTADOS UNIDOS DO BRAZIL * 1889 *". 21 stars for the federation.
Hardening: LOW-MODERATE. The figure is reduced to a bust (desincorporação), but as inaugural issue it retains performative energy — the coin *enacts* the Republic rather than merely commemorating it. No weaponization, no armor, no military context. The barrete frígio is present as revolutionary signifier but already being absorbed into numismatic convention.
Verdict: PROMOTE — zero-point of the Brazilian numismatic series. Essential for the longitudinal analysis.

**SCOUT-422 — 1000 Réis Efígie da República, 1889**
Date: 1889 | Medium: Moeda (prata .917, 12,75 g, 30 mm) | Regime: FUNDACIONAL
Description: Higher-denomination inaugural issue. Same program as SCOUT-316: female bust in profile, barrete frígio (to be confirmed visually), "REPUBLICA DOS ESTADOS UNIDOS DO BRASIL" obverse, "ORDEM E PROGRESSO" reverse. Larger module (30 mm) offers more detailed rendering.
Hardening: MODERATE. Same fundacional profile as SCOUT-316 but the higher purity (.917) and larger size suggest the State invested more material substance in the allegory at this value level. Desincorporação (bust only) is high, but the metal's brilliance resists full monocromatização of later, debased series. No martial attributes.
Verdict: PROMOTE — completes the inaugural trinity with SCOUT-316 and SCOUT-413.

**SCOUT-413 — 20.000 Réis Ouro, 1889–1922**
Date: 1889–1922 (entire First Republic) | Medium: Moeda (ouro) | Regime: FUNDACIONAL
Description: Highest face value of the Republic. Female profile with barrete frígio on obverse; reverse with "ORDEM E PROGRESSO," "15 DE NOVEMBRO DE 1889," and Cruzeiro do Sul constellation surrounded by 21 stars. Designer: Rodolfo Bernardelli; engraver: Ernest Paulin Tasset (French).
Hardening: MODERATE. Desincorporação extrema (head/profile only), monocromatização (gold), serialidade máxima (industrial minting). Yet the barrete frígio and the founding date retain the fundacional bond. The French engraver confirms the transatlantic circulation — the Brazilian Republic's face is literally cut by a French hand.
Verdict: PROMOTE — the apex of the numismatic hierarchy. The same effigy circulates in gold (elite), silver (commerce), and copper-nickel (popular).

**SCOUT-125 — 2000 Réis, 1906–1913**
Date: 1906–1913 | Medium: Moeda (prata .900) | Regime: NORMATIVO
Description: Bust of Republic in left-facing profile with barrete frígio. Circular legend: "REPUBLICA DOS ESTADOS UNIDOS DO BRASIL" + year. Reverse: coat of arms with "ORDEM E PROGRESSO" and "2000 REIS." Designer: Augusto Girardet (attributed).
Hardening: MÉDIO (explicitly scored by the scout). The figure has now been stabilized for mass circulation. The silver purity has dropped from .917 (1889) to .900. The barrete frígio survives as a domesticated attribute — revolutionary symbol now functioning as bureaucratic identifier. Rigidez postural is high, uniformização facial is advancing. No weaponization, no military context, no muscularity.
Verdict: PROMOTE — key midpoint in the 1889→1965 longitudinal. Documents the moment when the effigy becomes infrastructure rather than proclamation.

**SCOUT-119 — 1000 Réis, 1906–1912**
Date: 1906–1912 | Medium: Moeda (prata .900, 26 mm, 10 g) | Regime: NORMATIVO
Description: Female bust in left-facing profile with barrete frígio and Roman-style laurel crown. Inscription: "REPUBLICA DOS ESTADOS UNIDOS DO BRAZIL" with year flanked by stars. Reverse: Cruzeiro do Sul surrounded by 21 stars.
Hardening: MÉDIO. Same NORMATIVO pattern as SCOUT-125: calm, static bust, serialidade máxima, body reduced to essential attributes (barrete + laurel). The scout note explicitly observes that the effigy has been "despolitizada em relação à fase fundacional." This is bureaucratic, not martial, hardening.
Verdict: PROMOTE — fills the 1000 Réis slot in the denominational series.

**SCOUT-418 — 100 Réis Liberty MCMI, 1901**
Date: 1901 | Medium: Moeda (cobre-níquel, 5 g, 21 mm) | Regime: NORMATIVO
Description: Female effigy facing right, with star crown (states of the federation), no barrete frígio visible in this denomination. In frame of stars. Inscription: "REPUBLICA DOS ESTADOS UNIDOS DO BRASIL," "15 DE NOVEMBRO DE 1889," "MCMI."
Hardening: HIGH. All 10 indicators active. The smallest denomination: maximum serialidade, maximum desincorporação (head + neck only), maximum dessexualização (no body), maximum monocromatização. The star crown replaces the barrete frígio — the revolutionary symbol is substituted by a federative-heraldic one. This is bureaucratic purification in its purest form.
Verdict: PROMOTE — the lowest denomination of the MCMI series, proving that smaller value = greater hardening.

**SCOUT-419 — 200 Réis Liberty MCMI, 1901**
Date: 1901 | Medium: Moeda (cobre-níquel, 8 g, 25 mm) | Regime: NORMATIVO
Description: Same program as SCOUT-418 but in larger module (25 mm). Identical face to 100 Réis, confirming the deliberate strategy of uniformização facial across denominations.
Hardening: HIGH. Identical profile to SCOUT-418. The larger surface allows better observation of the genericized, idealized features. No weapon, no armor, no severity — the hardening is entirely in the register of serial production and facial standardization.
Verdict: PROMOTE — confirms that the MCMI series operates by identical repetition across three denominations.

**SCOUT-420 — 400 Réis Liberty MCMI, 1901**
Date: 1901 | Medium: Moeda (cobre-níquel, 12 g, 30 mm) | Regime: NORMATIVO
Description: Highest denomination of the MCMI base series. Largest module (30 mm) provides the most detailed version of the effigy. Double temporal anchoring: "15 DE NOVEMBRO DE 1889" + "MCMI."
Hardening: HIGH but paradoxical. The largest module offers the most detailed face — yet this very detail reveals the generic idealization. The founding date now functions as memory, not enactment. The scout note contrasts this with the French Semeuse: "Semeuse mostra corpo inteiro em movimento (FUNDACIONAL residual), enquanto a Liberty brasileira é reduzida a busto estático (NORMATIVO pleno)."
Verdict: PROMOTE — completes the MCMI triad. Essential for the Brazil-France comparison.

**SCOUT-103 — 50 Cruzeiros, 1965**
Date: 1965 | Medium: Moeda (cupro-níquel) | Regime: NORMATIVO
Description: Profile bust of the Republic, inspired by actress Tônia Carreiro. Designer: Benedito Ribeiro. The effigy here has an individualized model — a real woman's face — yet is rendered in the same numismatic profile convention.
Hardening: MÁXIMO (scored as "todos os 10 indicadores ativos"). This is the endpoint of the Brazilian numismatic longitudinal: 76 years of progressive purification. The paradox of using a specific actress's face for a genericized allegory is revealing — the individual is absorbed into the serial. No martial, muscular, or armored elements. The hardening is entirely bureaucratic.
Verdict: PROMOTE — the terminal point of the 1889→1965 arc. Indispensable.

### 2.2 Print/Periodical Republic Figures

**SCOUT-425 — Alegoria da República Brasileira, Revista Illustrada (Agostini), 1889**
Date: 1889 | Medium: Litografia em periódico | Regime: FUNDACIONAL
Description: Angelo Agostini's iconic post-Proclamation image. Female figure with barrete frígio (red Phrygian cap), dynamic body, holding the green-and-yellow Brazilian flag. Translation of the French Marianne model into Brazilian national colors.
Hardening: BAIXO/NONE. This is the Pathosformel fundacional — the body in its most alive state. Full figure, dynamic posture, revolutionary energy. The barrete frígio is RED — active political signifier, not yet domesticated. The adoption of the national flag (+610.1, +610.3) inaugurates the specifically Brazilian extension of the Marianne template. No weaponization; the body's power is symbolic, not martial.
Verdict: PROMOTE — the foundational Pathosformel. Zero-point of the Brazilian allegorical tradition.

**SCOUT-426 — Capa da Revista Illustrada n. 566 (Agostini), 1889**
Date: 1889 | Medium: Litografia (capa de periódico) | Regime: FUNDACIONAL
Description: Cover of the same issue. Likely the same or closely related composition as SCOUT-425. The scout notes that while the visual vocabulary is FUNDACIONAL, the weekly seriality of 4,000 copies already initiates the hardening process through mass reproduction.
Hardening: BAIXO (fundacional visual vocabulary) with nascent serialidade. The tension between fundacional content and mass-medium context is significant.
Verdict: PROMOTE — forms a pair with SCOUT-425. The seriality of the periodical medium (4,000 copies/week) is the hardening vector here.

**SCOUT-417 — O Dia de Amanhã, O Mequetrefe, 1889**
Date: 1889 (pre-Proclamation) | Medium: Litografia em periódico | Regime: FUNDACIONAL
Description: Young female figure in classical dress with barrete frígio, presented as a prophetic vision of the coming Republic. Posture is solemn but fluid — dynamic and revolutionary. The image operates as visual prophecy before the political fact.
Hardening: BAIXO. Young, beautiful, dynamic body. The figure's function is seduction — making the Republic desirable and inevitable. No weapons, no armor, no severity. The beauty of the body is the political argument.
Verdict: PROMOTE — unique as pre-Proclamation prophecy. Documents the allegorical body as anticipatory instrument.

**SCOUT-414 — A República nos braços de Floriano, 1891**
Date: 28 November 1891 | Medium: Litografia (Revista Illustrada n. 644) | Regime: NORMATIVO
Description: Marshal Deodoro da Fonseca, aged and exhausted, hands the female figure of the Republic (tunic + barrete frígio) into the arms of Marshal Floriano Peixoto, vigorous and uniformed. The Republic is depicted as a PASSIVE object transferred between two military men.
Hardening: MÉDIO but with a unique inflection. THIS IS THE ONE FILE WHERE MILITARY CONTEXT IS CENTRAL — yet the Republic figure is NOT the militarized agent. She is the fragile civil body being "protected" by military custody. The hardening operates through her objectification: she does not fight; she is handed over. No weapons in her hands, no armor, no muscularity — the martial power belongs entirely to the male figures. This is an inversion of what "classical purification" would predict: the Republic figure is made MORE passive by proximity to the military, not hardened.
Verdict: PROMOTE — critical for the argument that Brazilian Republic hardening is NOT martial but bureaucratic/objectifying. The military context subordinates rather than hardens the female allegory.

**SCOUT-416 — A República em Leilão, 1904**
Date: 17 September 1904 | Medium: Caricatura (O Malho n. 105) | Regime: NORMATIVO
Description: The Republic figure is being auctioned off by politicians. She appears passive, almost as merchandise — the sovereignty symbol becomes a commodity in the oligarchic system (allusion to the Sorocabana Railway auction).
Hardening: MÉDIO. The figure has tunic and barrete frígio but is completely passive. This is hardening through degradation: the revolutionary body is emptied of political content and inserted into the logic of market exchange. No severity, no martial posture — the opposite: commercialization as a form of purification.
Verdict: PROMOTE — documents the oligarchic phase of Republic iconography. The body-as-merchandise is an important variant of bureaucratic purification.

**SCOUT-427 — Alegoria da Republica, Manoel Lopes Rodrigues, 1896**
Date: 1896 | Medium: Óleo sobre tela, 230 x 120 cm | Regime: NORMATIVO
Description: Commissioned by the Bahia state government. The Republic is seated on a throne, with white pacifying color, sword as latent (not brandished) power, barrete frígio domesticated with laurel and coffee (tropical attribute). Full programmatic allegory.
Hardening: HIGH NORMATIVO. The seated posture, the latent sword (not brandished — "potência latente"), the white color, and the coffee attribute mark this as a fully institutionalized Republic. This IS the closest the Brazilian Republic comes to classical severity — the sword is present but resting. The figure is enthroned, authoritative but not aggressive. The coffee replaces the wheat of the French Semeuse: tropical domestication of the European template.
Verdict: PROMOTE — the most complete painted allegory of the Brazilian Republic. The latent sword is the only weapon in the entire cluster; its "not-brandished" state is significant.

### 2.3 Cédulas and Selos (Paper Money and Stamps)

**SCOUT-423 — 50 Mil Réis Cédula Banco do Brazil, 1890**
Date: 1890 | Medium: Cédula (papel-moeda, Waterlow & Sons, Londres) | Regime: FUNDACIONAL
Description: Tripartite female allegorical program: Liberty (central bust), Government (seated left), Justice (seated right). Printed by Waterlow & Sons, London. Three female bodies on a single banknote compose the complete founding promise of the new State.
Hardening: BAIXO-MODERADO. Paradox: three full bodies (low desincorporação), yet the symmetrical composition, guilloché framing, and European printing introduce serialidade and heraldicização. The bodies are present but already disciplined by the ornamental architecture of the banknote. No weapons beyond the probable sword of Justice. The colonial dimension is explicit: British engravers drawing the body of the Brazilian Republic.
Verdict: PROMOTE — the most complete founding iconographic program. The European printing introduces colonialidade do ver.

**SCOUT-410 — Selo Cabeça da Liberdade, Thesouro Nacional, 1891**
Date: 1891 (continued to 1918+) | Medium: Selo postal | Regime: FUNDACIONAL → NORMATIVO
Description: First republican stamp series. Female Liberty/Republic head, profile or 3/4, derived from the Marianist tradition. Multiple variants: with star, larger head, etc. The series spans 1891–1918+, documenting the transition from fundacional to normativo.
Hardening: PROGRESSIVE. The longitudinal 1891→1918 shows increasing desincorporação (full body → head → stylized profile) and increasing serialidade. The "Liberty and Star" variant explicitly replaces revolutionary attributes with heraldic ones. No weaponization — the head alone is sufficient for postal circulation.
Verdict: PROMOTE — the postal equivalent of the numismatic series. The longitudinal within a single item type is valuable.

**SCOUT-311 — Série Personalidades e Alegoria da Liberdade, 1906–1917**
Date: 1906–1917 | Medium: Selo postal (American Bank Note Co.) | Regime: NORMATIVO
Description: Mixed definitive series: LOW values carry female Liberty effigy (barrete frígio); HIGH values carry male portraits (Benjamin Constant, Floriano Peixoto). Printed in the USA. Gender hierarchy is mapped onto face-value hierarchy: the female allegory is the "small change" of the Republic.
Hardening: YES — by COMPARTILHAMENTO DO ESPAÇO (space-sharing). The female allegory now divides the definitive stamp with male portraits. This spatial coexistence is a form of bureaucratic hardening: the allegorical body is demoted to the lowest denominations while real male figures occupy the high values. The American printing adds colonial mediation.
Verdict: PROMOTE — the gender/denomination mapping is analytically unique.

## 3. Comparative Analysis: Brazil vs. France

### 3.1 The Brazilian Republic Does NOT Harden Through Weaponization

The central finding of this survey is that the Brazilian Republic figure shows virtually NO signs of "classical purification" in the martial sense. Across the entire cluster:

- **Muscularity**: Absent. The Brazilian Republic figure remains slender, idealized-classical, never athletic or muscular.
- **Weaponization**: Nearly absent. Only SCOUT-427 (Lopes Rodrigues) includes a sword — and it is explicitly described as "latent," resting, not brandished. No other file shows the Republic holding weapons.
- **Martial posture**: Absent. The figure sits (SCOUT-427), stands passively (SCOUT-414, SCOUT-416), or is reduced to a bust (all coins). There is no advancing, commanding, or defensive stance.
- **Severity/severitas**: Limited. The numismatic profiles achieve a kind of generic severity through facial uniformization, but this is bureaucratic — the blankness of the serialized coin, not the sternness of a commander.
- **Armor**: Absent. The figure wears classical tunic/draped cloth or is reduced to head-only.
- **Military context**: Only SCOUT-414 (República nos braços de Floriano) places the figure in military context — and there she is a PASSIVE object transferred between uniformed men. The military power belongs to the males; the allegory is the fragile civil charge they protect.

### 3.2 The Contrast with French Marianne

The French Marianne tradition, by contrast, contains a well-documented militarization vector:

- The barricade-Marianne of Delacroix (1830) already carries a rifle.
- The monumental Marianne of the 1889 Exposition (SCOUT-435) is NORMATIVO but still physically imposing.
- Marianne appears in multiple registers: revolutionary fighter, seated republican authority, productive Semeuse — the figure retains dynamic potential even in normativo phases.

The Brazilian Republic, born as a direct copy of Marianne (the barrete frígio is the explicit genetic marker), follows a divergent trajectory: she *de-dynamizes* without *militarizing*. Her hardening operates through:
1. Reduction to bust/head (desincorporação)
2. Mass reproduction on coins and stamps (serialidade)
3. Insertion into bureaucratic framing (heraldicização, inscrição estatal)
4. Material degradation (lower metal purity over time)

The Brazilian Republic becomes infrastructure, not commander.

### 3.3 National-Iconographic Specificity

This divergence can be explained by the specific conditions of Brazilian republicanism:

- The Brazilian Republic was proclaimed by a military coup (1889) but immediately sought civilian legitimacy through positivist ideology ("ORDEM E PROGRESSO"). The allegory's function was to naturalize the new order, not to celebrate revolutionary violence — which Brazilian republicanism explicitly lacked (no barricades, no popular uprising).
- The "República da Espada" (1889–1894) was a military government, but its visual program delegated military authority to male figures (Deodoro, Floriano) while keeping the female allegory as civil ornament — precisely as seen in SCOUT-414.
- The positivist ideology of Auguste Comte, enormously influential in Brazilian republicanism, associated the feminine with moral authority and the masculine with material force. The Republic figure is the moral face; the military is the material arm.

### 3.4 Temporal Trend: Does She Harden Over the 20th Century?

YES — but in a specific mode:

**1889 (Fundacional)**: Dynamic, full-bodied, flag-bearing, red barrete frígio. Political energy is visual energy (SCOUT-425, SCOUT-426, SCOUT-417).

**1896 (Normativo Emergent)**: Enthroned, seated, latent sword, white, domesticated with coffee. Authority replaces energy (SCOUT-427).

**1901–1913 (Normativo Pleno)**: Reduced to bust on coins. Barrete frígio persists but as bureaucratic identifier. Silver purity drops. Face becomes generic. Serialidade máxima (SCOUT-119, SCOUT-125, SCOUT-418/419/420).

**1965 (Normativo Terminal)**: Maximum hardening. All 10 indicators active. Tônia Carreiro's face absorbed into numismatic convention. The founding date is now pure commemoration, not enactment (SCOUT-103).

The temporal trend is clear: progressive **bureaucratic purification** — decoporation, serialization, facial uniformization, material degradation. But the trajectory does NOT lead toward militarization. The 1965 effigy is no more martial than the 1889 figure; if anything, she is less so.

### 3.5 The Catete Exception

SCOUT-406 (Palácio do Catete, 1899–1910) documents a different form of hardening: outright REMOVAL + REPLACEMENT. The seven female allegories on the presidential palace parapet were taken down in 1910 and substituted by eagles. This is not the Republic figure hardening — it is the Republic figure being ELIMINATED in favor of heraldic animals. This trajectory (body → eagle) parallels what SCOUT-ZW-11 documents for Weimar Germany (female allegory → eagle). It represents a limit case: the ultimate hardening is absence.

## 5. Key Findings

1. The Brazilian Republic cluster constitutes a coherent, temporally-distributed body of evidence spanning 76 years (1889–1965) across four media: coins, banknotes, stamps, and periodical prints.

2. The hardening of the Brazilian Republic figure is overwhelmingly BUREAUCRATIC rather than MARTIAL. She undergoes progressive decoporation, serialization, facial uniformization, and material degradation — but never acquires weapons, armor, muscularity, or martial aggression.

3. This distinguishes the Brazilian trajectory from the French Marianne, who can oscillate between revolutionary fighter and institutional authority. The Brazilian figure has no "barricade mode" — because the Brazilian Republic itself had no popular revolution.

4. The one military-context file (SCOUT-414) confirms rather than contradicts this pattern: the Republic figure is subordinated to male military power, not invested with it.

5. The SCOUT-ZW-24 panel (Metal vs. Paper) provides the strongest analytical framework: the material support partially determines the intensity of hardening, with metal coins imposing greater compression (and thus greater purification) than paper banknotes.

6. The Catete case (SCOUT-406) documents the limit phenomenon: not hardening of the figure but her ELIMINATION. This absence is itself a data point.

7. Future work: a dedicated SCOUT-ZW panel comparing Brazilian Republic (bureaucratic hardening) with French Marianne (martial + bureaucratic hardening) is recommended for Capítulo 4.
