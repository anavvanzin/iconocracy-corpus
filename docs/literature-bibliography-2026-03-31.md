## I. MARIANNE AND FRENCH REPUBLICAN ALLEGORY

**[CRITICAL]** AGULHON, Maurice. **Marianne au combat: l'imagerie et la symbolique republicaines de 1789 a 1880**. Paris: Flammarion, 1979.
- First volume of the foundational trilogy. Establishes the tension between "sage and classical" vs. "radical and red" visual registers. Maps directly onto FUNDACIONAL-to-NORMATIVO transition. Essential for *dessexualizacao* and *uniformizacao_facial*.

**[CRITICAL]** AGULHON, Maurice. **Marianne au pouvoir: l'imagerie et la symbolique republicaines de 1880 a 1914**. Paris: Flammarion, 1989.
- Documents Marianne's transformation from insurgent body to bureaucratic emblem -- mass-produced busts, standardized on coins and stamps. Core evidence for *serialidade*, *inscricao_estatal*, and *enquadramento_arquitetonico*.

AGULHON, Maurice. **Les metamorphoses de Marianne: l'imagerie et la symbolique republicaines de 1914 a nos jours**. Paris: Flammarion, 2001.
- Final volume completing the survey from WWI to the present.

AGULHON, Maurice. **Marianne into Battle: Republican Imagery and Symbolism in France, 1789-1880**. Translated by Janet Lloyd. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1981.
- English translation of the first volume.

**[CRITICAL]** HUNT, Lynn. **Politics, Culture, and Class in the French Revolution**. Berkeley: University of California Press, 1984.
- Key chapter on Hercules vs. Marianne. Under Jacobins, Marianne was active and armed; after Thermidor, seated and unarmed. Most explicit historical evidence for *rigidez_postural* and *desincorporacao* as political processes.

**[CRITICAL]** LANDES, Joan B. **Visualizing the Nation: Gender, Representation, and Revolution in Eighteenth-Century France**. Ithaca: Cornell University Press, 2001.
- Argues female allegories eroticized patriotism and bound male subjects to the nation-state. Essential for understanding *dessexualizacao* as a political operation.

**[CRITICAL]** GUTWIRTH, Madelyn. **The Twilight of the Goddesses: Women and Representation in the French Revolutionary Era**. New Brunswick: Rutgers University Press, 1992.
- Sections on "goddesses allegories," "the Maenad factor," and "imageries of the breast" provide direct evidence for FUNDACIONAL regime's bare-breasted Marianne and its subsequent suppression.

**[CRITICAL]** DE BAECQUE, Antoine. **The Body Politic: Corporeal Metaphor in Revolutionary France, 1770-1800**. Translated by Charlotte Mandell. Stanford: Stanford University Press, 1997.
- Three forms of political body representation: metaphysical, metaphorical, ceremonial. Directly supports *desincorporacao* as foundational gesture of republican iconocracy.

## II. FEMALE ALLEGORY -- GENERAL THEORY

**[CRITICAL]** WARNER, Marina. **Monuments and Maidens: The Allegory of the Female Form**. London: Weidenfeld and Nicolson, 1985.
- Central paradox: "Liberty is not represented by a woman because women were and are free." Theoretical foundation for endurecimento as a whole.

RIPA, Cesare. **Iconologia, overo Descrittione dell'imagini universali cavate dall'antichita et da altri luoghi**. Roma, 1593.
- Foundational emblem book that standardized allegorical personification. Indispensable primary source for *heraldicizacao*.

VAN STRATEN, Roelof. **An Introduction to Iconography: Symbols, Allusions and Meaning in the Visual Arts**. 2nd ed. London: Routledge, 1994.
- Authoritative introduction including ICONCLASS classification system (48C51).

## III. BODY, NATION, AND SEXUALITY

**[CRITICAL]** MOSSE, George L. **Nationalism and Sexuality: Middle-Class Morality and Sexual Norms in Modern Europe**. New York: Howard Fertig, 1985.
- "Ideal stereotype of male and female beauty" as normative disciplinary mechanism. Directly supports *dessexualizacao* and *uniformizacao_facial*.

MOSSE, George L. **The Nationalization of the Masses: Political Symbolism and Mass Movements in Germany from the Napoleonic Wars through the Third Reich**. New York: Howard Fertig, 1975.
- Sacralization of politics through monuments, festivals, mass liturgy. Key to *inscricao_estatal* and *enquadramento_arquitetonico*.

**[CRITICAL]** HOBSBAWM, Eric; RANGER, Terence (Eds.). **The Invention of Tradition**. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 1983.
- "Invented traditions" concept underpins the entire NORMATIVO regime.

## IV. LADY JUSTICE / JUSTITIA -- LEGAL ICONOGRAPHY

**[CRITICAL]** HAYAERT, Valerie. **Lady Justice: An Anatomy of Allegory**. Edinburgh: Edinburgh University Press, 2023.
- Anatomizes Lady Justice "organ by organ, limb by limb," analyzing thousands of images from 1400-1600. The anatomical method mirrors endurecimento indicator-by-indicator approach.

HAYAERT, Valerie. The Paradoxes of Lady Justice's Blindfold. In: HUYGEBAERT, Stefan et al. (Eds.). **The Art of Law**. Cham: Springer, 2018.
- Polysemic nature of the blindfold from Sebastian Brant's *Narrenschiff* (1494) to modern reinterpretation.

**[CRITICAL]** RESNIK, Judith; CURTIS, Dennis. **Representing Justice: Invention, Controversy, and Rights in City-States and Democratic Courtrooms**. New Haven: Yale University Press, 2011.
- 220+ images tracing courtroom decoration and Justitia statues. Essential for *enquadramento_arquitetonico*.

COVER, Robert M. Images of Justice. **Yale Law Journal**, v. 96, 1987.
- Seminal article on "critical ambiguity" of Justitia's blindfold.

**[CRITICAL]** SCHILD, Wolfgang. **Bilder von Recht und Gerechtigkeit**. Cologne: DuMont, 1995.
- Essential depictions of Justitia across centuries. Image of justice as "mirror of its respective time and culture."

## V. LAW, VISUAL CULTURE, AND EMBLEMS

**[CRITICAL]** GOODRICH, Peter. **Legal Emblems and the Art of Law: Obiter Depicta as the Vision of Governance**. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 2013.
- First comprehensive study of legal emblem books as juridical genre. "Visiocracy" concept -- closest existing theorization to "iconocracy."

GOODRICH, Peter. **Law in the Courts of Love: Literature and Other Minor Jurisprudences**. London: Routledge, 1996.
- Intersection of law, literature, rhetoric, and visuality in jurisprudence.

GOODRICH, Peter. Visiocracy: On the Futures of the Fingerpost. **Critical Inquiry**, v. 39, n. 3, pp. 498-531, 2013. DOI: 10.1086/670045.
- "Visiocratic regime" -- visual governance apparatus where sovereignty is transmitted through images, ceremony, and insignia.

GOODRICH, Peter. **Retinal Justice** (chapter in *Scopic Regime of Law*). Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2023.
- Latest elaboration: "retinal justice" as method of training the eye for visual law.

**[CRITICAL]** VISMANN, Cornelia. **Medien der Rechtsprechung**. Frankfurt: S. Fischer, 2011.
- Media-theoretical approach to law, including visual staging of legal authority.

VISMANN, Cornelia. **Files: Law and Media Technology**. Translated by Geoffrey Winthrop-Young. Stanford: Stanford University Press, 2008.
- Genealogy of law as filing/recording practice. Relevant for *inscricao_estatal*.

## VI. BRITANNIA, COLUMBIA, GERMANIA -- NATIONAL ALLEGORIES

DRESSER, Madge. Britannia. In: SAMUEL, Raphael (Ed.). **Patriotism: The Making and Unmaking of British National Identity**. London: Routledge, 1989.
- Britannia from Roman personification through Victorian imperial icon. Transition from spear to trident: direct evidence for MILITAR regime.

MILLAT, Gilbert. Britannia: Grandeur et infortune d'une allegorie nationale dans l'univers du cartoon britannique 1842-1999. **Revue LISA/LISA e-journal**, v. I, n. 1, pp. 4-23, 2003. DOI: 10.4000/lisa.3109.
- 400+ cartoons analyzed. Largest systematic empirical study of Britannia.

SCOTT, David. Marianne and Britannia -- Confronting National Icons. **L'image**, n. 2, pp. 151-163, 1996.
- Directly comparative (France/UK) on female national allegories.

BRANDT, Bettina. Germania in Armor: The Female Representation of an Endangered German Nation. In: WATANABE-O'KELLY, Helen; COLVIN, Sarah (Eds.). **Women and Death 2**. Camden House, 2010. pp. 86-109.
- Germania from captive Amazon on Roman coins to militant armored figure of 1848 and the German Empire.

TAYLOR, Miles. John Bull and the Iconography of Public Opinion in England 1712-1929. **Past and Present**, n. 134, pp. 93-128, 1992.
- Gender dynamics in competition between male (John Bull) and female (Britannia) national personifications.

MELLINI, Peter; MATTHEWS, Roy T. From Britannia to Maggie: The Rise and Fall of a National Symbol. **History Today**, v. 38, pp. 17-23, Sept. 1988.
- Allegory loses symbolic power as real women enter politics. Inverse relationship between allegory and real female political power.

## VII. WARBURG, PATHOSFORMEL, AND THE SURVIVAL OF ALLEGORICAL FORMS

**[CRITICAL]** WARBURG, Aby. **Bilderatlas Mnemosyne** [1924-1929]. Edited by Martin Warnke and Claudia Brink. Berlin: Akademie Verlag, 2000.
- *Pathosformel* -- "emotionally charged visual tropes that recur throughout images in Western Europe" -- is the direct methodological ancestor of endurecimento indicators. The *Nympha* is the prototypical Pathosformel. The thesis tracks how the Nympha becomes Justitia.

DIDI-HUBERMAN, Georges. **L'image survivante: histoire de l'art et temps des fantomes selon Aby Warburg**. Paris: Minuit, 2002.
- *Nachleben* (surviving image) explains how allegorical forms persist while undergoing transformation -- the theoretical basis for tracing endurecimento across regimes.

- Esp. Vol. 1: *Art in an Age of Revolution, 1750-1800* (1987). The 1971 article "The Second Republic's Contest for the Figure of the Republic" directly addresses competition between allegorical forms.

## IX. ICONOCLASM AND DESTRUCTION OF ALLEGORIES

GAMBONI, Dario. **The Destruction of Art: Iconoclasm and Vandalism Since the French Revolution**. New Haven: Yale University Press, 1997.
- Systematic study of art destruction from the French Revolution to the present. Essential for understanding how allegories are destroyed during political transitions.

VON TUNZELMANN, Alex. **Fallen Idols: Twelve Statues That Made History**. London: Headline Publishing, 2021.
- Twelve cases of monument toppling, construction and destruction.
