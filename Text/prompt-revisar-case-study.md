**Suggested opening move:**

> On 8 January 2023, when rioters inscribed »Perdeu, mané« across the chest of Alfredo Ceschiatti's *A Justiça*, they did not merely deface a statue. They performed an act of counter-literacy—a violent insistence that the abstract, universal Goddess of Justice was actually a partisan judge masquerading in stone. This moment exposes the central paradox of modern legal orders: their legitimacy depends entirely on images they cannot control. By force of law, the state responded to this graffiti as an attack on democracy itself. In doing so, the judiciary confirmed what the iconoclast already knew—that images are not supplements to law, but its very foundation.
