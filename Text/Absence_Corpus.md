# The Absence Corpus — Structured Negatives

## Core idea

Systematic study of what's NOT in the corpus: 106 items, zero Black allegories of Justice, zero Indigenous Mariannes, zero male Justices. The corpus proves a negative as powerful as the positive findings.

## Structured absences by category

- **Race:** No Black/Indigenous/Asian allegorical figures representing Justice, Liberty, or the Republic in official state iconography
- **Gender inversion:** No male personification of Justice in the 19th-20th century corpus (despite ancient precedents)
- **Non-Western traditions:** No Dharma, Ma'at, or other non-Greco-Roman justice personifications in Western state iconography
- **Age:** No elderly, no child allegories — always young adult female
- **Disability:** No allegorical figure with visible disability (blindfold is symbolic, not disability)

## Table format for thesis

| Absence | Expected if universal | Actually found | What this proves |
|---------|----------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Black Justitia | Yes | 0/106 | Whiteness is constitutive, not incidental |
| Male Justitia | Possible | 0/106 | Feminization is structural, not decorative |
| Indigenous Marianne | Yes (in BR) | 0/11 BR | Positivist racial exclusion (documented in BR-005) |

## Connection to BE-CONGO-1912

The Congo banknote proves the point by exception: when a Black female body IS present, it is NOT allegorized — it remains ethnographic. Only white femininity achieves allegorical sublimation.
