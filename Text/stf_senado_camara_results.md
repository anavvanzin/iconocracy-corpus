### STF Item 1

**Title:** "A Justiça" (exterior / granito)  
**Artist/Creator:** Alfredo Ceschiatti (1918, Belo Horizonte, MG – 1989, Rio de Janeiro, RJ)  
**Date:** 1961 (inaugurated at STF's opening in Brasília, 1960)  
**Location:** Exterior — Praça dos Três Poderes, in front of the STF building, Brasília, DF  
**Material:** Monolithic granite block from Petrópolis, RJ  
**Dimensions:** 3.3 m height × 1.48 m width  
**Direct URL (institutional reference):** `https://portal.stf.jus.br/` (no direct digital record page found); Facebook post: `https://www.facebook.com/supremotribunalfederal/photos/1362046729300646/`  
**Wikimedia Commons category with 62 images:** `https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Justi%C3%A7a_(Alfredo_Ceschiatti)`  
**Sample Wikimedia image (downloadable):**
- `https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:A_Justi%C3%A7a_escultura_BSB_08_2005_24v.jpg` (1,000 × 1,250 px, 1.27 MB — photographer: Mariordo / Mario Roberto Durán Ortiz)
- `https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:A_Justica_Alfredo_Ceschiatti_Brasilia_Brasil.jpg` (1,672 × 2,268 px, 1 MB — photographer: Morio, 2005)
- `https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Supreme_Federal_Court_-_Justice_Statue.jpg` (3,744 × 5,616 px, 11 MB)

**Agência Senado / Flickr photo:**  
URL: `https://www.flickr.com/photos/agenciasenado/46826464635`  
Photographer: Pedro França/Agência Senado (2019)

**Iconographic description:**  
Seated female figure representing Têmis (Themis), goddess of Justice in Greek mythology. Eyes blindfolded (venda), symbolizing impartiality. Sword (espada) resting across her lap, symbolizing force, courage, order, and the rule of law. No scales (balança) — a notable absence, interpreted as placing the balance of justice in citizens' hands. Carved from a single granite block. No base inscription. Monumental civic sculpture in the tradition of neoclassical allegory updated to Brazilian modernism.

**Historical notes:**  
Vandalized during the January 8, 2023 attack on Brasília's democratic institutions; attacked again November 13, 2024. The STF restoration laboratory undertook repairs in 2023.

### STF Item 3 (CCJF — old STF building, Rio de Janeiro)

**Title:** Four historical figure paintings in the Sala de Sessões (old STF plenary room)  
**Building:** Former Supremo Tribunal Federal (1909–1960), now **Centro Cultural Justiça Federal (CCJF)** — Av. Rio Branco, 241, Centro, Rio de Janeiro, RJ  
**Date of building inauguration:** April 3, 1909  
**Date of paintings' discovery:** During restoration in the late 1990s  

**The four panels depict the following figures:**

| Panel | Subject | Iconographic relation | Status |
|-------|---------|----------------------|--------|
| 1 | **Imperador Justiniano** (Justinian I) | Symbolizes Justice (Justiça) | Recovered |
| 2 | **Cônsul Ápio Cláudio** (Appius Claudius) | Symbolizes Legality (Legalidade) | Recovered |
| 3 | **Marco Túlio Cícero** (Cicero) | Symbolizes Civil Peace (Paz civil) | Recovered |
| 4 | **Quinto Múcio Cévola** (Quintus Mucius Scaevola) | Symbolizes non-abuse of law | Identified Dec. 2025 via competition |

**Note on allegorical content:**  
The panels represent male historical figures of Roman law/jurisprudence, not female allegories. Each figure is associated with a Latin phrase inscribed below: Justiniano → justiça; Ápio Cláudio → legalidade; Cícero → paz civil; Cévola → "Abusus non est usus, sed corruptela" (abuse is not use, it is corruption). The 4th panel's figure was degraded and only recently identified (December 2025) via the "Prêmio Quis Ego Sum" competition by CCJF/Caixa Econômica Federal.

**References:**  
- Migalhas article (July 2025): `https://www.migalhas.com.br/quentes/434880/concurso-premiara-quem-identificar-pintura-na-antiga-sede-do-stf`  
- Result announcement (December 2025): `https://www.migalhas.com.br/quentes/446328/figura-apagada-em-parede-do-antigo-stf-e-identificado-veja-quem-e`  
- TRF2 official announcement: `https://www.trf2.jus.br/trf2/noticia/2025/misterio-desvendado-na-sala-de-sessoes-do-ccjf`

**Assessment for this research:**  
The CCJF Sala de Sessões paintings are iconographically significant but depict historical male legal figures, not female allegories. No female allegorical paintings have been documented in the CCJF's former STF session hall. The STF's early iconographic program was oriented around Roman legal authority figures rather than female virtues.

### Senado Item 1

**Title:** "Dama da República" (personalidade/thematic entry)  
**Artwork title:** "O Retrato da República"  
**Artist/Creator:** Décio Villares (1851–1931)  
**Date:** Not specified on Tainacan page; Villares active in the 1880s–1910s  
**Location:** Tainacan Senado — Personalidades section  
**Direct URL:** `https://tainacan.senado.leg.br/personalidades/dama-da-republica/`  
**Image download URL:** Not available on the Tainacan page (no img URLs found in page source)  

**Iconographic description:**  
Alegoria republicana influenced by French tradition. Depicts a female figure known as "Marianne," representing liberty. The figure incorporates symbolic republican elements. Marianne (the French feminine personification of the Republic) became the model for Brazilian republican iconography post-1889. Villares was one of the most prominent painters of the Proclamação da República (November 15, 1889). The Marianne figure includes the barrete frígio (Phrygian cap), colors of republican symbolism, and attributes of freedom.

**Contextual note:**  
This Tainacan entry is a "personalidade" (biographical/thematic entry) rather than a catalogued artwork with full provenance. It links to the concept of Marianne as republican allegory, citing Décio Villares as the creator of "O Retrato da República." The painting itself may be held in a different collection (possibly at the Museu da República or private collection); the Senado Tainacan entry appears to be interpretive/contextual rather than a direct catalog of a work owned by the Senate. Related items: D. Pedro II, Deodoro da Fonseca.

**Iconographic description:**

Donated to the Senado as a prerequisite for the use of exhibition space.

**Artist/Creator:** Alfredo Ceschiatti (1918–1989)  
**Date:** 1970 (acquired by Senado in 1970; commissioned for the Senate)  
**Location:** Salão Nobre, Senado Federal, Brasília (displayed in front of a large red panel by Athos Bulcão)  
**Material:** Bronze  
**Dimensions:** 1.50 m × 47 cm × 35 cm  
**Direct URL (Senado institutional article):** `https://www12.senado.leg.br/institucional/museu/bloco-noticias-destaques/ceschiatti-o-escultor-das-mulheres-sensuais-e-da-capital-da-republica`

**Iconographic description:**  
Female nude figure (mulher nua) in Ceschiatti's characteristic style: curvilinear, rounded forms derived from Renaissance tradition with a touch of Baroque (Mineiro). Not an allegorical figure per se — this is a sensual nude female form, not depicting Justice or Republic. Commissioned exclusively for the Senate. Part of the larger program of Ceschiatti/Niemeyer integration of art and modernist architecture in Brasília. The work is displayed against a vibrant red panel by Athos Bulcão, creating a notable visual pairing.

**Title:** "Sem título" (commonly known as "Anjo")  
**Artist/Creator:** Alfredo Ceschiatti (1918–1989)  
**Date:** 1977  
**Location:** Salão Verde (Green Hall), Câmara dos Deputados, Palácio do Congresso Nacional, Brasília  
**Material:** Bronze fundido (cast bronze)  
**Dimensions:** 180 × 163 × 83 cm  
**Direct URL (Câmara official page):** `https://www2.camara.leg.br/a-camara/visiteacamara/cultura-na-camara/museu/acervo/obras-de-arte/alfredo-ceschiatti/alfredo-ceschiatti-1918-belo-horizonte-mg-2013-1989-rio-de-janeiro-rj`  
**Câmara TV episode:** `https://www.camara.leg.br/tv/cultura-na-camara/1108426-cultura-na-camara-o-anjo-de-alfredo-ceschiatti/`  
**Google Arts & Culture (Câmara):** `https://artsandculture.google.com/story/conhe%C3%A7a-a-c%C3%A2mara-dos-deputados-camara-dos-deputados-brasil/yAXRUCLCJZHQPg?hl=en`  
**Pulsar Imagens licensed photo:** `https://www.pulsarimagens.com.br/foto/Escultura-em-bronze-de-Alfredo-Ceschiatti-intitulada-Anjo---Sal%C3%A3o-Verde-do-Congresso-Nacional`

**Iconographic description:**  
Half-body bronze sculpture of an angel (anjo) in cast bronze. This is Ceschiatti's only work in the Câmara collection. The sculpture closely resembles the aluminum angels hanging in the Catedral Metropolitana de Brasília (also by Ceschiatti), differing in material (bronze here vs. aluminum there) and scale. The figure is described as a "torso-level angel" (escultura de meio corpo de um anjo), with rounded, serene features typical of Ceschiatti's style. Not strictly a Justice or Republic allegory — an angelic figure — but iconographically related to allegorical female-coded divine/virtuous figures. Commissioned as part of Oscar Niemeyer's 1970s decoration project for the Salão Verde.

**Note:** Although angel figures are not gendered as strictly female in the Ceschiatti tradition, the Brasília cathedral angels are rendered in an androgynous to feminine style consistent with the artist's preference for curvilinear female forms.

### Câmara — Historical Allegory Context

The Câmara dos Deputados does not appear to hold paintings of female Justice or Republic allegories in its publicly documented collection. The primary allegorical artworks at the Congresso Nacional are:
- The two Ceschiatti A Justiça sculptures (both at STF, not the Câmara)

## KEY FINDINGS AND GAPS

### Primary Female Justice Allegories
The most significant female allegorical representations of Justice found are:
1. **STF exterior granite "A Justiça" (1961)** — the canonical Brazilian justice allegory; best-documented; 62 images on Wikimedia Commons
2. **STF interior bronze "A Justiça" (1978)** — second Ceschiatti work; less photographed; STF's own channels are the best source
3. **Senado "Justiça Sentada" (2018)** — catalogued on Tainacan with downloadable images; full Justitia attribute set (venda, balança, espada)

### Primary Female Republic Allegories
1. **Senado "Dama da República" / Décio Villares** — Tainacan entry exists but has no image; the painting itself may not be physically held by the Senate
2. The broader Marianne/República iconographic tradition is well-documented in academic literature but no other Republic allegory paintings were found in the three institutions' publicly accessible digital collections

### Limitations and Gaps
- **STF Memória portal** blocks automated access; the Museu Sepúlveda Pertence virtual tour (`bit.ly/tourvirtual-24`) may contain additional items not accessible via web research
- **Senado Tainacan** has limited metadata on many items; "Baixar" links exist for Justiça Sentada and Princesa Isabel but direct file URLs require clicking through the platform
- **Câmara CEDI** has no public digital art catalog; direct inquiry or on-site research would be required to identify any Republic allegory paintings in the collection
- The CCJF (old STF in Rio) Sala de Sessões panels are male legal figures, not female allegories; no female allegorical paintings were found in that building's documented holdings
