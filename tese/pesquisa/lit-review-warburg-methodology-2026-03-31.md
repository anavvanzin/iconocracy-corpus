### Research Question

How can Aby Warburg's concepts of Pathosformel, Nachleben, and Bilderatlas Mnemosyne be operationalized as methodology for analyzing mass-produced juridical imagery (coins, stamps, monuments) rather than fine art? What precedents exist for feminist, postcolonial, and digital extensions of Warburg's method?

## Axis 1: Pathosformel — The Core Concept

### 1. JOHNSON/BECKER (2013, 19 cit.)
**"Aby Warburg's Pathosformel as Methodological Paradigm."** *The Journal of Art Historiography.*

**THE central source for this thesis.** Already fichado in Notion (Becker 2013 fichamento). Uses Germania as case study to demonstrate Pathosformel across disciplines. Key argument: Pathosformel = "an emotionally charged visual trope" that survives across centuries and media. The paper demonstrates the method on national personification — exactly what the thesis extends to 6 countries.

**Thesis connection:** The ICONOCRACY thesis extends Johnson/Becker by:
- Going cross-national (6 countries vs. 1)
- Going cross-support (5 media vs. fine art only)
- Introducing 10 measurable indicators to operationalize the Pathosformel

### 2. CASTAÑOS (2019, 4 cit.)
**"The pathos formulae and their survival."** *Comparative Cinema.*

Applies Pathosformel to a contemporary photographic image — demonstrates the concept works for modern media, not just Renaissance art. Key for the thesis: if Pathosformel applies to photography, it applies to coins and stamps.

### 3. Study of Image by Aby Warburg (2022)
**Kimiya-ye-Honar.**

Traces Warburg's intellectual genealogy: Nietzsche's Apollonian-Dionysian, Vischer's empathy theory, Burckhardt's cultural totalism. Key insight: "Expressive gesture analysis offers an unusual way of looking at past shapes and allows the audience to find images of the past in the present." This is the theoretical basis for tracking Marianne/Britannia/Germania across 200 years of coins.

### 4. Nachleben der Antike: Morphology and History (2020)
Warburg's key quote (1924 library inauguration): "The question of the influence of Antiquity is merely a phenomenological reflection of a much more individual problematic... individual memory of ancient images loaded with vital power and expressive energy."

**For the thesis:** The allegorical figures on coins ARE "ancient images loaded with vital power" — Marianne descends from Roman Libertas, Britannia from Roman Britannia on Hadrian's coins, Justitia from Greek Themis. The Nachleben is literal.

## Axis 2: Bilderatlas Mnemosyne — Method as Form

### 5. JOHNSON (2012, 103 cit.)
**"Memory, Metaphor, and Aby Warburg's Atlas of Images."** *Modern Language Review.*

The definitive English-language monograph on the Atlas. Key: the Atlas is "not simply the culmination of Warburg's lifelong study but the ultimate expression of his search for syncretic solutions." The Atlas makes "visible the many polarities that fueled antiquity's afterlife."

**For the thesis:** The 8 Atlas panels (Gênese → Justitia → Domesticação → ENDURECIMENTO → Pedra e Bronze → Balança e Império → Branquitude → Fissuras) ARE the thesis's Bilderatlas. Each panel is a Warburgian arrangement of images revealing polarities.

### 6. MORETTI / Totentanz (2017, 20 cit.)
**"Operationalizing Aby Warburg's Pathosformeln."** *New Left Review.*

**CRITICAL for the thesis methodology.** Franco Moretti and collaborators attempt to "operationalize" the Pathosformel — to turn it into "a sequence of quantitative operations, or in other words, into an algorithm." They analyze Panel 45 of Mnemosyne for spatial patterns.

**For the thesis:** The 10 purification indicators ARE an operationalization of the Pathosformel. Where Moretti uses computational image analysis, the thesis uses ordinal coding (0-4 scale) of morphological features. Both are attempts to make Warburg quantitative.

### 7. MANOVICH / Images as Data (2016, 15 cit.)
**"Cultural Analytics and Aby Warburg's Mnemosyne."**

Compares Warburg's "iconology of intervals" with contemporary data visualization. Argues that "contextual and image color data knowledge should continue to be important for digital art history." Case study: Panel 45, color analysis of violence representations.

**For the thesis:** The Zwischenraum (interval between images) is the thesis's core analytical unit — each ZW panel is a Warburgian interval. The ENDURECIMIENTO score gives each interval a quantitative dimension.

### 8. Digitising the Mnemosyne Atlas (2020, 4 cit.)
**Theory, Culture & Society.**

Problematizes the "simplified relation between repetition and replication" in digital Warburg. Argues digitization can reveal "discrepancies between individual entities and larger collective patterns."

**For the thesis:** The digital corpus (corpus-data.json, webiconocracy, companion app) IS a digitized Bilderatlas. The question of repetition/replication is central: does a coin stamped 440 million times (Semeuse) have the same Nachleben as a unique painting?

## Axis 3: Feminist and Postcolonial Extensions

### 9. SILVERMAN (2018, 1 cit.)
**"A Feminist Picture Atlas: Images of Lactation in Medieval and Early Modern Art."** *Early Modern Women.*

Adapts Warburg's Pathosformel and Nachleben to investigate "the afterlife of images of lactation" — a gendered Warburg method. Key: "Warburg cherished the art object as anthropological trace, capable of harboring, communicating, and re-instantiating an assemblage of meanings and histories."

**For the thesis:** If Silverman can build a feminist picture atlas for lactation, the thesis can build one for the female allegorical body on state imagery. The ICONOCRACY Atlas is a feminist Bilderatlas.

### 10. MAZZUCCHELLI (2018)
**Refugee Atlas modeled on Mnemosyne.**

Uses Warburg's Atlas format for contemporary refugee imagery — demonstrates the method works for political subjects, not just art history. Precedent for using Bilderatlas form to analyze power relations.

### 11. Queer Pathos (2023)
Applies Pathosformel + queer theory — anachronistic gestures as "queer symptom." Demonstrates that the Pathosformel concept can absorb critical theory (feminist, queer, postcolonial) without losing analytical power.

### 12. Warburg's Atlas as Iconoclasm (2022)
**European Review.**

Argues the Atlas "aims to eliminate magic and reveal the hidden plot that seeks to dominate society." First panel: zodiac constellations → Europe → Medici banking family genealogy = "Renaissance elites accumulate wealth because it is seemingly dictated by the stars."

**For the thesis:** The ICONOCRACY Atlas similarly reveals the "hidden plot": states use female bodies to naturalize sovereignty. The 10 purification indicators are the analytical tools for revealing this mechanism.

## Methodological Implications for the Thesis

### The thesis's Warburgian architecture

| Warburg concept | Thesis implementation |
|---|---|
| **Pathosformel** | The gesture formula of each national allegory (Marianne sowing, Britannia trident-bearing, Germania helmeted, etc.) |
| **Nachleben** | The survival of these formulas across 200 years and 5 support types |
| **Bilderatlas** | The 8 thesis panels (Gênese → Fissuras), each a constellation of corpus items |
| **Zwischenraum** | The interval between images = the thesis's analytical unit (ZW-01 to ZW-23) |
| **Denkraum** | The "space for contemplation" = the companion app, where panels can be explored |
| **Polarities** | Each ZW panel stages a polarity (FUNDACIONAL ↔ MILITAR, presence ↔ absence, metropole ↔ colony) |

### The 10 indicators as operationalized Pathosformel

Warburg never quantified the Pathosformel. Moretti (2017) attempted computational operationalization. The thesis proposes a **human-coded ordinal operationalization**: the 10 purification indicators measure HOW a Pathosformel mutates (hardens/softens) across regimes. The ENDURECIMENTO score is the mean mutation index.

This positions the thesis at the intersection of:
- Warburg studies (theoretical framework)
- Digital humanities (data infrastructure)
- Feminist visual culture (critical lens)
- Legal iconography (domain expertise)
