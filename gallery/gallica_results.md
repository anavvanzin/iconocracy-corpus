## Iconographic Context

### Marianne / République

According to the [BnF classes resource on Marianne](https://classes.bnf.fr/pdf/Revol2_Marianne.pdf), the allegory is characterized by:
- **Bonnet phrygien** (Phrygian cap / bonnet rouge / bonnet de la liberté): the central attribute, red like blood; derived from Cesare Ripa's *Iconologia* (1593/1636)
- **Pique surmontée du bonnet**: pike topped with the freedom cap — per the official seal description of 25 September 1792
- **Faisceau d'armes** (fasces/bundle of arms): attribute of force and unity, often at her side
- **Gouvernail** (rudder): symbol of direction/governance, at her feet
- **Drapeau tricolore**: she brandishes the tricolore, standing in full figure, often half-nude
- Classical drapery in the Greco-Latin tradition, drawing from depictions of Liberté

During the Third Republic (1870–1940), Marianne takes on generous rural forms (Demeter/Ceres), entering town halls from 1877. Under WWI propaganda, she appears on war-loan posters. Post-1946, she becomes a more middle-class consensual figure.

Per the [EHNE encyclopedia entry on female allegories](https://ehne.fr/fr/encyclopedie/thématiques/genre-et-europe/genre-et-révolution-en-europe-aux-xixe-xxe-siècles/les-allégories-féminines-de-la-nation), the name "Marianne" was rare until the mid-19th century; the figure circulated as Liberté, République, or simply France since 1789. The red-bonneted Marianne became codified with the Third Republic proclamation (4 September 1870).

### Justice

The female personification of Justice (Justitia) derives from Roman tradition and Cesare Ripa's *Iconologia*. Key attributes:
- **Balance** (scales): the balance of judgment
- **Glaive / épée** (sword): enforcement of law; sometimes "flamboyante" (flaming)
- **Bandeau sur les yeux** (blindfold): impartiality — though this becomes codified more clearly from the 16th century onward
- **Sceptre**: authority
- Classical drapery; often paired with Vérité (Truth) and Vengeance in allegorical compositions

### Liberté

Per the BnF Marianne source: directly derived from Ripa's figure of **Liberté** — woman dressed in white, sceptre in right hand, bonnet in left hand, cat at feet (cat omitted by revolutionaries). In Revolutionary France:
- **Bonnet phrygien** (identical with Marianne)
- **Sceptre de la Raison** (sceptre of Reason)
- She tramples **Ignorance** and **Fanatisme**
- The faisceau (fasces) also appears
- Often depicted with an obelisk or altar of the fatherland in the background
